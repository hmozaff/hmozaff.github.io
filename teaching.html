Teaching Statement

My teaching philosophy is centered on clarity, inclusiveness, and motivation. I strive to create learning environments where students from diverse backgrounds feel supported, challenged, and confident in applying engineering concepts. I emphasize intuitive explanations, practical examples, and a supportive learning culture that encourages curiosity and independent problem-solving.

Teaching Philosophy

My approach follows a simple structure: motivation → problem framing → guided discovery → practice → synthesis. Students learn best when they understand why a concept matters and how it connects to real-world engineering. I use demonstrations, hands-on activities, guided discussions, and step-by-step problem solving to build confidence and deepen conceptual understanding.

I continually refine my teaching by gathering student feedback and adapting course materials to match student needs, ensuring an inclusive classroom where students gain confidence and develop strong analytical skills.

Teaching Experience

I develop syllabi, lectures, assignments, exams, and laboratory modules and supervise hands-on student projects.
