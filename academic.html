Academic Background

I am a dual-background Ph.D. researcher in Robotics, Control Systems, and Autonomous Intelligence, specializing in nonlinear estimation, multi-sensor fusion, motion planning, and delay-compensated filtering frameworks (EKF/UKF/CKF). My research focuses on mathematically rigorous and computationally efficient algorithms for real-world autonomous robotic systems.

  • Autonomous Robotics • Motion Planning • Optimal Control
  • AI & Learning-Based Control for Robotics

Expanded Research Profile

My research integrates theoretical control and estimation with real-world robotics. I develop algorithms that remain robust despite noise, delay, environmental uncertainty, and real-time constraints. Recent work includes:

  • Developing augmented measurement–lead models that remove state-growth in delayed systems, enabling efficient EKF/UKF/CKF estimation.
  • Designing multi-sensor fusion systems combining LiDAR, IMU, and visual data for accurate localization and mapping.
  • Creating motion planning frameworks (MPC, CHOMP, TrajOpt) for safe robot navigation.
  • Researching learning-based planning and control for autonomous ground vehicles.
