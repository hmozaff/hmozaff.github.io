Academic Portfolio

Research Overview

My academic work focuses on delayed-state Bayesian estimation, nonlinear filtering, human-centered autonomy, and robust control of dynamic systems. I investigate how measurement delays, asynchronous sensing, and nonlinearities affect the stability and observability of autonomous systems.

My contributions include delay-compensated EKF/UKF/CKF formulations, quaternion-constrained orientation estimation, and motivational human driver modeling. I aim to build mathematically rigorous and computationally efficient frameworks for perception and autonomy in real robotic platforms.

Publications

  • Human Driver Motivational Model for Rear-End Collision Avoidance — Mozaffari, Nahvi.
  • A Motivational Driver Steering Model: Task Difficulty Homeostasis — Mozaffari, Nahvi.
  • Human Driver Model for Autonomous Vehicle Analysis — Mozaffari, Nahvi.

Academic Projects

  • Delay-Aware EKF/UKF/CKF Framework: A reorganized estimation structure allowing nonlinear filtering under measurement delays without increasing state dimension.
  • Quaternion-Constrained UKF: Orientation estimation with norm stabilization and robust propagation under noise and disturbances.
  • Motivational Human Driver Model: A psychologically-inspired driver model for autonomous vehicle simulation and crash avoidance analysis.
