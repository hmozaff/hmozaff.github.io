Academic Profile

Academic Background

I am a dual-background Ph.D. researcher in Robotics, Control Systems, and Autonomous Intelligence, with specialization in nonlinear estimation, multi-sensor fusion, motion planning, and delay-compensated filtering frameworks (EKF/UKF/CKF). My research aims to develop mathematically rigorous and computationally efficient algorithms for real-world autonomous robotic systems.

Research Interests

  • State Estimation & Nonlinear Filtering (EKF, UKF, CKF)
  • Sensor Fusion using LiDAR, IMU, Camera, and Multi-modal Systems
  • Time-Delay Systems & Delay Compensation in Robotics
  • Autonomous Robotics • Motion Planning • Optimal Control
  • AI & Learning-Based Control for Autonomous Systems

Expanded Research Profile

My research integrates theoretical control and estimation with practical autonomous systems. I focus on designing algorithms that remain robust despite sensor noise, delay, environmental uncertainty, and real-time computational constraints. Examples of my recent work include:

  • Developing augmented measurement–lead models to eliminate state-growth in delay systems, enabling efficient application of EKF/UKF/CKF.
  • Designing multi-sensor fusion pipelines combining LiDAR, IMU, and vision systems for high-accuracy robotics localization.
  • Creating robust motion planning frameworks using MPC, CHOMP, and TrajOpt for safe navigation in uncertain environments.
  • Investigating learning-enabled control for autonomous ground vehicles integrating behavior prediction and adaptive control.

My long-term research vision is to develop perception–estimation–planning algorithms that unify mathematical rigor with industrial deployability, bridging the gap between academic theory and practical robotics applications.
