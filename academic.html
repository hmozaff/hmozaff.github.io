Research Overview

My academic work focuses on delayed-state Bayesian estimation, nonlinear filtering, human-centered autonomy, and robust control of dynamic systems. I study how measurement delays, asynchronous sensing, and nonlinearities influence stability, observability, and performance in autonomous robotic systems.

I develop delay-aware EKF/UKF/CKF formulations, quaternion-constrained filters for orientation estimation, and motivational human driver models grounded in control theory. My goal is to design mathematically rigorous and computationally efficient estimation frameworks deployable on real robotic platforms.
