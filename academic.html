Academic Portfolio

Research Overview

My academic work focuses on delayed-state Bayesian estimation, nonlinear filtering, and human-centered autonomy for robotic and dynamic systems. I study how measurement delays, asynchronous sensing, and nonlinearities influence system stability, observability, and estimation accuracy.

I develop delay-aware EKF/UKF/CKF formulations, quaternion-constrained filters, and motivational human driver models inspired by cognitive psychology. My goal is to build mathematically rigorous and computationally efficient estimation frameworks deployable on real robotic platforms.

Research Projects

  • Delay-Aware EKF/UKF/CKF Framework: A reorganized nonlinear estimation architecture enabling stable inference under measurement delays without increasing state dimension.
  • Quaternion-Constrained UKF: A robust orientation estimation method with norm stabilization and improved propagation under noise and disturbances.
  • Motivational Human Driver Model: A psychologically inspired driver model for AV simulation, risk assessment, and crash-avoidance control design.
